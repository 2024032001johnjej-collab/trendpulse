SECTION 1 — UNDERSTANDING THE PROBLEM STATEMENT

What Problem Are You Solving?

Problem Statement PS-04 asks teams to build a Social Media Trend & Sentiment Dashboard. This is a real-world problem that every brand, PR agency, product team, and political campaign faces daily. Here is the core challenge:

Modern organizations are BLIND to what is happening on social media in real time. They discover crises after the fact, spend hours on manual monitoring, and have no unified place where trend detection + sentiment scoring + instant alerts come together.

The Three Pain Points

  • Delayed Awareness — Brands miss viral trends until too late. 72% of brand crises start on social media; avg response window is 4-6 hours.
  • Manual Overload — Monitoring posts manually is slow, error-prone, unscalable. Manual monitoring misses 60% of spikes.
  • No Unified View — No single dashboard combines trend detection, sentiment scoring, and real-time alerts in one place.

Why This Problem Matters to Judges

Brand reputation is built over years and destroyed in hours. A single viral negative post can cost a company millions. TrendPulse is not a toy dashboard — it is a crisis prevention system. Frame it this way during your demo.

SECTION 6 — HOW TO WIN: ENHANCEMENT STRATEGIES

What Separates Winners from Participants

Must-Have Enhancements (High Impact, Low Effort)

  • Live Demo Spike Trigger Button — Add a hidden admin button that injects 20 negative posts at once. Press it during demo → alert fires → crisis score jumps → judges gasp. Pre-plan this as your big moment.
  • Animated Crisis Score Gauge — Use Chart.js gauge or D3 arc to show the 0-100 score visually. Watching the needle swing from Safe to Crisis is memorable and screenshot-worthy.
  • Recommended Action Panel — Based on crisis score, display dynamic text: 'Issue a public statement', 'Engage top 5 negative commenters', 'Alert PR team'. This is your AI-powered decision support layer.
  • Comparative Trend View — Show two hashtags side-by-side (#iPhone vs #Samsung). Makes the product feel like a real competitive intelligence tool.

Stretch Enhancements (If Time Permits)

  • Telegram / Email Alert Integration — Use python-telegram-bot or Flask-Mail to send a real notification when crisis fires. Live phone notification during demo = instant wow factor. Takes ~45 minutes.
  • Sentiment Confidence Score — Show DistilBERT confidence % alongside each post (e.g., 'Negative — 94% confident'). Reinforces real AI, not keyword matching.
  • Entity Recognition — Use spaCy to extract brand names, people, and places from posts. Show 'Top Mentioned Entities' alongside the word cloud.
  • Export Report Button — 'Download PDF Report' button that exports the current dashboard snapshot. Makes TrendPulse feel like enterprise SaaS.

Presentation Tips That Win Prizes

The #1 mistake hackathon teams make: they demo features, not stories. Tell THIS story: 'It is Monday morning. A negative tweet goes viral. TrendPulse detects it in 2 seconds, fires an alert, computes a Crisis Score of 78, and tells your team exactly what to do. Without TrendPulse, you would have found out 4 hours later — too late.'

  • Open with: '72% of brand crises start on social media. Most companies find out 4 hours too late.' Then say 'We built the solution.'
  • Narrate the demo: 'Notice the crisis score just jumped from 31 to 74 — the system detected a sentiment spike and is now recommending we issue a public statement.'
  • Close with scale: 'In a real deployment with Twitter API, TrendPulse monitors 10,000 posts per minute — enterprise-ready on day one.'
  • When judges ask 'what if AI gets it wrong?' — say: 'VADER is 80% accurate on social text. DistilBERT pushes to 91%. We flag borderline cases for human review — by design.'

SECTION 9 — WHAT NOT TO DO

Common Hackathon Mistakes to Avoid

  • DO NOT try to connect to live Twitter/Reddit APIs. Rate limits, OAuth, and approval times will waste 2-3 hours. Your simulated dataset is smarter.
  • DO NOT train a model from scratch. Pre-trained VADER + DistilBERT is the correct choice. Training is for production, not hackathons.
  • DO NOT add features after Hour 9. Hours 9-12 are for testing, polish, and rehearsal — not new code.
  • DO NOT have all 4 members work on the same file. Git conflicts at midnight are a disaster. Divide clearly by component.
  • DO NOT skip the demo rehearsal. Teams that rehearse twice win. Teams that wing it lose.
  • DO NOT let the demo run silently. Narrate every action. Judges watch 20 demos — help them understand yours.
